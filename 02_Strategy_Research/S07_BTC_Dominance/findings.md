# Findings

## Research Outcome

The BTC Relative Strength strategy produced the strongest performance observed during the research process.

The strategy achieved:

- Sharpe Ratio: 1.561
- Equity: 8020
- Max Drawdown: -87.8%

This exceeded the performance of both traditional momentum and regime-aware momentum strategies.

---

## What Worked

### Relative Strength Improved Momentum

Rather than measuring momentum in isolation, the strategy measured momentum relative to Bitcoin.

This successfully identified assets demonstrating leadership within the cryptocurrency ecosystem.

---

### Bitcoin Functioned as a Market Benchmark

The results support the hypothesis that Bitcoin should be treated as the dominant market factor.

Assets outperforming Bitcoin consistently generated superior risk-adjusted returns.

---

### Regime Filtering Remained Effective

The Bitcoin 200-day moving average filter continued to improve portfolio robustness by avoiding prolonged bear markets.

---

## Key Insight

Absolute momentum is useful.

Relative momentum versus Bitcoin is even more useful.

The strongest opportunities were not necessarily the assets rising the fastest, but the assets outperforming Bitcoin itself.

---

## Connection to Earlier Research

This result directly validates:

- Finding 6: BTC Dominance
- Finding 3: Market Regime Impact
- Finding 1: Momentum Persistence

The strategy combines all three effects into a unified framework.

---

## Research Conclusion

Bitcoin acts as both:

1. The dominant market factor.
2. The appropriate benchmark for evaluating cryptocurrency leadership.

Measuring performance relative to Bitcoin appears superior to measuring performance in absolute terms.

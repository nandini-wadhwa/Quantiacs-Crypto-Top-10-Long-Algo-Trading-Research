# Findings

## Research Outcome

The Tail Risk Timing strategy generated positive risk-adjusted returns but failed to outperform the strongest momentum-based strategies.

The strategy achieved:

- Sharpe Ratio: 0.888
- Equity: 51.1
- Max Drawdown: -94.0%

This confirms that extreme price moves contain predictive information but are not a sufficiently strong standalone source of alpha.

---

## What Worked

### Tail Events Contain Information

Assets experiencing unusually large returns continued to exhibit above-average performance.

The strategy successfully monetized part of the tail-event effect identified during exploratory analysis.

---

### Positive Standalone Performance

The strategy generated a Sharpe ratio close to 0.9 despite relying on a single factor.

This validates the hypothesis that tail events are not purely random.

---

## What Did Not Work

### Turnover Became Excessive

The strategy produced the highest turnover observed during the research process.

Average turnover exceeded 0.73 while average holding periods fell below two days.

---

### Drawdowns Remained Severe

Although tail events contained useful information, they were insufficient to protect the portfolio during adverse market environments.

---

### Signal Was Noisy

Large price moves were not consistently followed by continuation.

Some events developed into trends while others quickly reversed.

---

## Key Insight

Tail events appear to identify market attention and information arrival.

However, they do not provide a reliable indication of future direction.

The signal contains information but lacks consistency.

---

## Research Conclusion

Tail-event behaviour represents a useful secondary factor but is unlikely to outperform momentum or BTC-relative-strength signals as a primary source of alpha.

The factor may be most valuable when combined with directional signals.

# Findings

## Research Outcome

The BTC Relative Strength and Volume combination failed to outperform BTC Relative Strength alone.

The strategy achieved:

- Sharpe Ratio: 1.544
- Equity: 8145.8
- Max Drawdown: -87.4%

---

## What Worked

### Strong Performance Was Maintained

The strategy preserved most of the performance characteristics of BTC Relative Strength.

Returns remained among the strongest observed during the research process.

---

### Volume Did Not Significantly Harm Results

Although Sharpe declined slightly, overall performance remained robust.

This suggests volume contains useful information, even if it does not materially improve asset selection.

---

## What Did Not Work

### Volume Added Limited Incremental Alpha

The primary objective was to determine whether volume provided information beyond BTC Relative Strength.

The results suggest that any incremental contribution is small.

Performance remained slightly below S07.

---

## Key Insight

BTC Relative Strength appears to capture most of the information that volume contributes to asset selection.

Assets attracting capital tend to already be identified through relative strength.

---

## Research Conclusion

Volume functions better as a supporting confirmation factor than as a primary source of alpha.

The evidence does not support allocating significant weight to volume in future portfolio models.

# Findings

## Research Outcome

The BTC Relative Strength and Momentum combination failed to improve upon BTC Relative Strength alone.

The strategy achieved:

* Sharpe Ratio: 1.561
* Equity: 8020
* Max Drawdown: -87.8%

Performance was effectively identical to S07.

---

## What Worked

### Strong Performance Was Maintained

The strategy preserved the strong performance characteristics of BTC Relative Strength.

Returns, drawdowns, turnover, and holding periods remained almost unchanged.

---

### Momentum Did Not Harm Performance

Adding momentum did not significantly degrade results.

The combined strategy remained one of the strongest strategies developed during the research process.

---

## What Did Not Work

### Momentum Added No Incremental Alpha

The primary objective of the strategy was to determine whether Momentum and BTC Relative Strength represented independent sources of alpha.

The results indicate that they do not.

Performance was statistically identical to S07.

---

## Key Insight

BTC Relative Strength already incorporates most of the useful information contained within traditional momentum signals.

Assets outperforming Bitcoin are generally the same assets exhibiting the strongest absolute momentum.

---

## Research Conclusion

BTC Relative Strength appears superior to traditional momentum as a cryptocurrency-specific factor.

The evidence suggests that BTC Relative Strength largely subsumes the predictive power of absolute momentum.

Future research should focus on combining BTC Relative Strength with factors that capture different information, such as volume or market structure.

---
